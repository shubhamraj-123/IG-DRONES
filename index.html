Jagannath Temple

The Jagannath Temple, located in Puri, Odisha, is one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites in India. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, it is renowned for its annual Rath Yatra, where deities are paraded on massive chariots.

India Gate

New Delhi: The India Gate is an iconic monument in New Delhi. Built in 1931, the monument commemorates the Indian soldiers who died in World War I and is a popular tourist attraction.

Taj Mahal

Agra, Uttar Pradesh: The iconic white-marble mausoleum is an architectural marvel and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Built in the 17th century by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan, the Taj Mahal is a symbol of love and devotion.

Mahabodhi Temple

Bodh Gaya, Bihar: The Mahabodhi Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the city of Bodh Gaya. The temple is associated with the life of Gautama Buddha and is an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists.

Sun Temple

Konark, Odisha: The Sun Temple is a 13th-century temple dedicated to the Hindu god Surya. The temple is an architectural masterwork and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Red Fort

Delhi: The Red Fort is a historic fortification in the heart of Delhi. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century, it is a symbol of India's rich history and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Tirupati Temple

The Tirumala Venkateswara Temple in Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh, is one of the most sacred and visited Hindu pilgrimage sites in India. Dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Vishnu, the temple is renowned for its grandeur and attracts millions of devotees annually.
